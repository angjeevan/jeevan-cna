About Course

21CSE477T-Cloud Native Architecture for Modern Platforms is a professional elective course is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the architectural principles behind modern application platforms and the best high-level structures for various application types, including web, database, and thick-client systems.

Gap Between Syllabus & Industrial Needs

Case Study Report Background and Scenario Proposed Advanced Solution
NanCo Art Services Company NanCo Art Services operates the "ArtPort" application suite (Gallery, Museum, and Consumer versions). As the company expanded globally, users reported severe scalability and performance issues. The applications were built as in-house monoliths, making them expensive to update and slow to load. The "Refactor and Augment" Strategy Instead of a simple "Lift and Shift," NanCo should adopt a Refactor strategy, breaking the monolith into microservices while using advanced single-node patterns to bridge the gap
